Urban Heat Island Effect

Rapid urbanization, loss of vegetation, and concentrated human activity are making cities significantly hotter than their surrounding areas. This Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect creates numerous problems:

  • Increased energy consumption for cooling
  • Worsened air quality and pollution
  • Higher health risks, especially for vulnerable populations
  • Reduced quality of life and outdoor comfort

Unsustainable Solutions

Traditional cooling methods like air-conditioning are part of the problem:

  • Massive energy consumption
  • Increased CO₂ emissions
  • Heat exhaust that further warms urban environments
  • High costs for residents and municipalities
